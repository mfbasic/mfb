NAME
  civil - build a DateTime from civil parts in a zone

SYNOPSIS
  datetime::civil(date AS Date, time AS Time, zone AS Zone) AS DateTime

PACKAGE
  datetime

IMPORTS
  IMPORT datetime

DESCRIPTION
  datetime::civil builds a DateTime by interpreting a calendar Date and a
  wall-clock Time as a local time in zone, resolving the UTC offset that applies
  to that local moment, and returning the canonical projection of the resulting
  Instant back through zone. Because the result is the projection of a concrete
  Instant, it round-trips: datetime::resolve on the returned DateTime recovers
  the same Instant, and that Instant projected through zone with
  datetime::inZone reproduces the same DateTime fields.

  The date's year, month, and day and the time's hour, minute, and second are
  combined into a count of seconds for that wall-clock moment, treated as a
  civil (zone-local) time. The offset for that moment is then resolved from
  zone. For a zone with a fixed offset (built by datetime::utc or
  datetime::fixedOffset) the offset is constant. For the host's local zone
  (datetime::local) the offset is resolved from the platform's zone table at the
  named instant, so the result is daylight-saving correct.

  When the named local time does not exist or is not unique because of a
  daylight-saving transition, civil resolves it deterministically using the
  lenient convention. To detect a transition it probes the zone's offset one day
  before and one day after the named local time, bracketing any single nearby
  transition. If both probes agree, that offset is used directly. If they
  differ, the moment lies near a transition: a spring-forward gap, where the
  named local time is skipped, shifts forward by the size of the gap onto the
  post-transition offset; a fall-back overlap, where the named local time occurs
  twice, picks the earlier (pre-transition) offset.

  The time's sub-second nanoseconds are carried through unchanged into the
  resulting Instant and DateTime; only the whole-second civil fields participate
  in offset resolution.

  datetime::civil is pure: it reads no host state beyond what zone itself
  resolves and has no side effects.

PARAMETERS
  date AS Date
    The calendar date (year, month, day) of the wall-clock moment, interpreted
    as a local date in zone.

  time AS Time
    The wall-clock time of day (hour, minute, second, nanos) of the moment,
    interpreted as a local time in zone. The nanos field is preserved in the
    result.

  zone AS Zone
    The zone in which date and time are read as local civil fields and against
    which the UTC offset is resolved. May be a fixed-offset zone (datetime::utc,
    datetime::fixedOffset) or the host's local zone (datetime::local).

RETURN VALUE
  AS DateTime
    The canonical DateTime naming the same moment as date and time in zone,
    carrying the resolved UTC offset. The value round-trips through
    datetime::resolve and datetime::inZone. For a spring-forward gap the fields
    reflect the forward-shifted instant; for a fall-back overlap they reflect
    the earlier of the two candidate offsets.

ERRORS
  No errors.

EXAMPLES
  Combine a date and time into a DateTime in the local zone:

    IMPORT datetime

    LET d AS Date = datetime::date(2026, 6, 26)
    LET tm AS Time = datetime::time(9, 30)
    LET dt AS DateTime = datetime::civil(d, tm, datetime::local())

  Build a DateTime in UTC and recover its Instant:

    IMPORT datetime

    LET d AS Date = datetime::date(2026, 1, 1)
    LET tm AS Time = datetime::time(0, 0)
    LET dt AS DateTime = datetime::civil(d, tm, datetime::utc())
    LET t AS Instant = datetime::resolve(dt)
